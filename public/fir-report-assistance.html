FIR Filing Guide

Step-by-step process

1

Gather Your Documents

Collect your Aadhar / government ID, screenshots of the incident, transaction records (if fraud), and any chat messages or email evidence.

2

Visit the Nearest Station (or File Online)

Go to the station we've found for you, or file at cybercrime.gov.in for cyber offences. Mention you want to "lodge an FIR" — not just a complaint.

3

Describe the Incident Clearly

State the date, time, location of incident, what happened, and any accused/account details. Be factual and sequential. The officer will record your statement.

4

Receive Your FIR Copy (Free of Cost)

Under Section 154 CrPC, you are entitled to a free copy of your FIR. Insist on this. Note the FIR number — you'll need it to track the case.

5

If Police Refuse to File

You can approach the Superintendent of Police (SP) or file a complaint before a Magistrate under Section 156(3) CrPC. Contact the Women Helpline (1091) for gender-based cases.

6

Track Your Case

Use your state police portal or the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal to track the status. Keep a copy of all communication.

This guide provides general procedural information only. For legal advice specific to your situation, consult a qualified advocate. TruthGuard is not liable for case outcomes.
